The Hidden Lever: The Spice Tax Crisis

The Hook: In 1490, a pound of nutmeg in Europe cost more than its weight in gold.

The Reason: When the Ottoman Empire took over Constantinople in 1453, they locked down the traditional overland Silk Road. They slapped massive tariffs on European merchants. If Europeans wanted pepper or cinnamon, they had to pay exorbitant prices to Middle Eastern middlemen.
