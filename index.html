History Unlocked

Early 15th Century

Portuguese Sailors Brave the Open Atlantic

Portuguese captains began launching fleets down the unknown coast of Africa. They braved superstitions of sea monsters and boiling equatorial waters to map out entirely new ocean routes.

🔍 Why did they suddenly risk everything for alternative routes?

The Hidden Lever: The Spice Tax Crisis

The Hook: In 1490, a pound of nutmeg in Europe cost more than its weight in gold.

The Reason: When the Ottoman Empire took over Constantinople in 1453, they locked down the traditional overland Silk Road. They slapped massive tariffs on European merchants. If Europeans wanted pepper, cinnamon, or medicine, they had to pay exorbitant prices to Middle Eastern middlemen.

The Result: Portugal realized it was financially smarter to risk their entire navy exploring uncharted oceans than to keep bleeding cash to foreign empires.
